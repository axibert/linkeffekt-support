1 · What is LinkEffekt?

LinkEffekt streams audio between music apps and devices, over your local network (via Ableton Link Audio) or over the internet (Online mode). It ships as three AUv3 plugins that run inside any AUv3 host (AUM, Logic Pro, Cubasis, Drambo, GarageBand and others):

PluginTypeUse it to
LinkEffekt SendAudio effectStream a track's audio out. Insert it on the track you want to share.
LinkEffekt ReceiveAudio effectReceive channels. Works in every host; this is the standard receiver.
LinkEffekt Receive InstrumentInstrumentThe same receiver as a sound source, for hosts that need one on the channel (e.g. as the source in AUM) or for Logic multi-output routing.

What can you do with it?

  • Jam with two devices in one room: each player’s iPad/iPhone streams its tracks to the others over Wi-Fi, beat-locked via Ableton Link (§2, §7).
  • Route audio between apps on one device: e.g. send a track from AUM into Cubasis to record it — no Wi-Fi, no setup, lowest latency (§2, “Same device or same host”).
  • Bring a remote friend into the session: stream over the internet, peer-to-peer and encrypted; their audio locks onto your bars (§3, §6).
  • Record iOS audio in Ableton Live: Live 12.4+ receives LinkEffekt Send directly as a Link Audio input (§7, FAQ).
  • Monitor a whole band on one device: several Send channels, summed in one Receive — or one Receive per channel for separate faders (§5).

Free vs. Unlimited Channels: the free version includes 1 Send and 1 Receive channel, fully functional on the local network. The one-time in-app purchase Unlimited Channels unlocks unlimited instances, monitoring multiple channels at once, and the Online (internet) mode. Purchase or restore it in the LinkEffekt app or directly inside the plugins.

First step on iOS: open the LinkEffekt app once after installing. This triggers the Local Network permission. Without it, the plugins cannot see each other.

2 · Quick start: local network (LAN)

You need two separate apps or devices: Send and Receive share one network identity inside the same host app, so they cannot connect to each other within a single host. Two devices, or two host apps on one device (e.g. Cubasis sends, AUM receives), both work.

  1. Sender: insert LinkEffekt Send on a track. Tap the gear and enable Ableton Link and Share Audio. The orange banner disappears when sharing is active.
  2. Receiver: load LinkEffekt Receive on a track in the other app/device (same Wi-Fi network).
  3. In the receiver, open the Now Receiving menu and pick the sender's channel.
  4. You should hear audio and see the meter move. The status line reads:
Receiving audio.

Latency on one device: most of the end-to-end latency comes from the two host apps' audio buffer sizes, not from LinkEffekt (its own buffer starts at 3 ms on direct paths). For the tightest timing set both apps to 128 or 256 frames (AUM: Settings > Buffer Size; Cubasis: Setup > Audio > Latency). The Receive plugin shows the measured breakdown under Advanced timing: total, LinkEffekt's share, and the host apps' share.

Tip: name your Send channels (the Name field). That name is what receivers see, and each additional Send instance appears as its own channel.

Same device or same host — app-to-app routing

Everything on one iPad/Mac? LinkEffekt routes these cases over a direct fast path that needs no Wi-Fi, no Local Network permission and no Ableton Link setup — it is the lowest-latency, most stable option:

  • A sender in another app on the same device appears as a channel tagged This device.
  • A sender inside the same host app (e.g. AUM track 1 → track 2) appears as This host.

Example — record an AUM setup into Cubasis: insert LinkEffekt Send on the AUM channel you want to capture, open Cubasis, insert LinkEffekt Receive on an audio track, pick the channel tagged This device, arm the track and record. The same recipe routes a synth app through another host’s effects, or feeds a DJ app’s output into a recorder — anywhere iOS offers no direct audio path between apps.

3 · Quick start: Online (internet)

Online mode streams over the internet, peer-to-peer, encrypted end-to-end (DTLS). It requires Unlimited Channels on both sides.

  1. Person A: in LinkEffekt Send, switch Destination to Online, tap Create Room and share the room code (copy button next to the code).
  2. Person B: in LinkEffekt Receive, switch the top selector to Online, enter the code and tap Join.
  3. B picks A's channel from Now Receiving. For two-way audio, B also loads a Send (or uses Send my track, see §6).
  4. With Beat Sync on (default), press play on your host transport on both sides and set both hosts to the same tempo. The peer's audio locks onto your beat grid, delayed by whole bars (see §7).

The header shows an ONLINE badge with the room code whenever an instance is in Online mode: green dot = peer connected, orange = waiting for a peer.

4 · LinkEffekt Send: reference

  • Name: the channel name receivers see. Renames propagate live, also in Online rooms.
  • Send Audio: master switch for streaming this track. Off = the plugin passes audio through locally but sends nothing.
  • Mute Local Output (default on): mutes the track locally after streaming, so you don't hear the signal twice (once dry, once through the receiver with latency). Turn it off only if the receiver is somewhere you can't hear.
  • Gain / Pan: applied to the stream (and to the local output), with click-free ramps. The bottom of the gain range is true silence.
  • Level meter: post gain/pan; red = clipping (tap to reset).
  • Destination: LAN & Local / Online: where this track streams (LAN & Local always includes listeners on this device). Switching Online to LAN & Local removes only this track from the room; the room itself stays open until you tap Leave (a "room is still open" line with a Leave button stays visible in the LAN & Local tab).
  • Gear (Ableton Link settings): the Link session, Share Audio (required for LAN sending; Online is independent of it) and the Link Offset slider (±100 ms) to fine-align audio that arrives consistently early or late. Negative offsets can cause dropouts on the receiver; raise the receiver's latency if needed.
  • Link Audio: Enabled/Disabled: status line, mirrors the Share Audio toggle.

Freemium: the second and further Send instances without Unlimited Channels keep passing audio through locally but do not stream ("Channel Limited" banner with an unlock button).

5 · LinkEffekt Receive: reference

  • LAN & Local / Online selector: which world the channel list below shows — LAN & Local covers network channels and the direct paths on this device (This host / This device). The header shows Link peers (LAN) or the room status (ONLINE badge).
  • Now Receiving: pick exactly one channel. "Channel (unavailable)" means the sender disappeared (app closed, network lost).
  • Monitor multiple channels (Unlimited Channels): sum several channels into this plugin's stereo output. Channels that are active but belong to the other transport tab are shown with a LAN/Local/Online tag so nothing is summed invisibly. For separate faders per channel, use one Receive instance per channel instead.
  • Sync status line (green metronome = locked and audio flowing): "In sync · 12 ms latency" shows the effective receive latency. On a direct connection (This device / This host) this is the measured end-to-end value (transit + buffer + host pipeline difference), so it can read higher than the Latency slider; "In sync · on the bar" means Bar Sync placement (whole bars, see §7); "Buffering…" is normal for a moment after selecting a channel; "Free-run: playing at lowest latency" means there is no shared beat reference (audio flows normally, just not beat-aligned); "Playing free-run … enable Ableton Link (gear icon)" tells you how to get beat-locked on LAN; "Waiting for your host transport. Press play" (Online) means the beat lock is just waiting for your host’s transport to run.
  • Bar Sync (toggle in the LAN & Local tab; Online is always bar-synced): locks the incoming downbeat to your bar: up to one bar late, but rock-solid even over a shaky connection. Off = lowest latency. Hosts with plugin delay compensation (AUM, Logic) align the low-latency stream automatically; hosts without it (Cubasis) keep a small constant offset (see §7).
  • Advanced timing (collapsed by default — the automation handles buffering by itself):
    • Latency (3-200 ms, default 10): how much audio is buffered — this is the lower bound; LinkEffekt auto-raises the effective value when the network needs more (shown as "Current: … (auto-adjusted)") and never lowers your setting on its own. On a direct connection (This device / This host) the automatic starts as low as 3 ms while the slider sits at its default — the status line shows the real value. In Bar Sync mode the slider has no fine effect (delay is whole bars). On direct paths a Measured line breaks the total down into LinkEffekt's buffer and the host apps' share (their audio buffer sizes plus output pipelines); lowering the buffer size in the host apps is the lever for the host share.
    • Nudge / Delay: fine-tunes where the received downbeat lands. With Bar Sync (or Online mode) the slider is bipolar (Nudge, ±100 ms) — a full bar of buffered audio gives room to play earlier or later. In low-latency mode the slider is Delay (0…+100 ms): audio can always be played later, but not earlier than the sender delivers it. Leave at 0 if it sounds right; a non-zero value is shown next to the "Advanced timing" label.
  • Mixer block, in signal order: Received (mute the incoming stream), Host track (Receive effect only; the track's own input, default muted to prevent feedback, see §8), and Output with master gain/pan/mute.
  • Diagnostics line: plain-language status under the channel menu (see §8 for what each message means).
  • Gear, then Settings: Ableton Link settings plus Live Mode (reports zero latency to the host so it skips delay compensation — for live playing/jamming only) and Show diagnostics (a technical sync readout under the channel list; useful when reporting an issue, off by default).
  • Packet statistics (gaps/duplicates) appear automatically only when the network has problems.

Receive vs. Receive Instrument: identical engine. Use the effect everywhere you can insert it; use the Instrument where the channel needs a source (AUM source slot, Logic software-instrument track with multi-output).

6 · Online mode in detail

  • One room per host app. All Online instances inside the same host share one room and one connection. The first instance creates/joins; the others follow automatically. Leave disconnects all of the host's online tracks (the dialog says so).
  • Multiple tracks: every Send-Online instance is its own stream. The peer sees each one as a separate channel (up to 8 per direction).
  • Send my track (Receive effect in Online mode): also streams this track's input into the room, giving full two-way audio in a single plugin slot. Built for hosts with only one usable slot, e.g. DJ apps.
  • Room codes are one-time invitations relayed by the signaling server; the audio itself flows peer-to-peer, DTLS-encrypted. No audio passes through our servers. Rooms with only one participant close after 30 minutes; active sessions have no time limit.
  • Reconnect: if one side drops out, rejoin with the same code while the other side is still in the room. If both left, create a fresh room.
  • Mono send (optional, in the Send settings) halves the bandwidth. Useful on weak connections.

7 · Beat Sync: how it works and what to expect

Audio that travels through a network always arrives late; no software can remove that. What LinkEffekt can do is make the delay musical:

  • LAN (low latency, Bar Sync off): the receiver places the incoming audio onto your local Ableton Link grid. The result is a stable, grid-locked offset roughly equal to the Latency setting, instead of drifting timing. Both apps must be in the same Link session (Link enabled on both).
  • Bar Sync (LAN) / Online: the delay is rounded up to whole bars on your host's beat grid. The incoming "1" lands exactly on your "1", typically one bar later (2 s at 120 BPM). Online requirements: Beat Sync on (default), host transport playing on both sides, same tempo on both hosts. When you press play, expect about one bar of silence while the buffer fills, then it locks.
  • Free-run is what you get without a shared beat reference: lowest latency, no alignment. Fine for monitoring, speech, or when you play by ear.
  • A constant offset on some hosts, and how to remove it completely. Everything can be perfectly in sync and the received audio still sits a small, constant amount late. That is the host delaying its own output after the plugin has already handed the audio over, without reporting it. No plugin can hear its host's speaker output, so this is the one thing LinkEffekt cannot measure for you. The good news: hosts that do it usually say by how much.
    • Cubasis 3, when it is the one RECEIVING: turn off Multi-core Processing. Setup, Audio. That setting is the extra buffer Cubasis puts behind the plugin. With it off, Cubasis reports a Guard buffer of 0 ms and the received audio is tight with no Nudge at all.
    • Cubasis 3, when it is the one SENDING: leave Multi-core Processing ON. That same buffer is what lets Cubasis hand its audio over evenly. Without it, Cubasis renders with no slack at all and delivers unevenly, which the receiver hears as crackling. Keep it on and take the offset back at the receiver with a positive Delay the size of the guard buffer.
    • Then leave the audio buffer generous. This is the counter-intuitive part: once Multi-core Processing is off, the audio buffer size no longer affects timing accuracy at all (it is already part of the host's own timestamp, which LinkEffekt compensates). So pick a buffer that keeps the engine comfortable rather than the smallest one. Best of both: Multi-core off, buffer generous.
    • If you must keep Multi-core on, read the number and nudge it back. Setup, Audio, tap the latency total and read Guard buffer. Set Nudge to minus that value (possible in Bar Sync or Online mode, where there is buffer to play earlier). Measured on device: a 21 ms guard buffer needed exactly Nudge −21, and after lowering the audio buffer to 2.7 ms the guard buffer fell to 11 ms and −11 was exact. When Cubasis is the sender the sign flips: its own output is late, so the receiver needs a positive Delay of the same size.
    • AUM has no such buffer and is tight out of the box, with Nudge at 0.
  • If the plugin suggests Bar Sync ("Timing looks shaky on this host"): the host's beat reporting is jittering. Bar Sync absorbs that completely; the suggestion is one tap, never automatic.
  • Recording into Ableton Live (LAN): enable Sync to Incoming Audio under Live's Settings → Link for sample-accurate placement; raise Live's Link-Audio latency slider until playback is stable, and set Monitor to In.

The two hosts' own metronomes are not aligned to each other over the internet. Each side hears the peer on their own grid. In a remote jam that is exactly what you want, since each player only hears their own device.

8 · Troubleshooting

No channel appears in Now Receiving (LAN)

  • Send and Receive must run in different apps or devices (§2).
  • Everything on one device? Look for the This device / This host tag — those channels work without Wi-Fi, network permission or Link (§2).
  • Same Wi-Fi network? Some routers isolate clients ("AP/client isolation"). Disable that, or use a hotspot.
  • Open the LinkEffekt app once (Local Network permission). The plugin shows an orange banner if this is missing.
  • On the sender: gear, then Share Audio must be on.

Channel selected, but no sound: read the diagnostics line

No audio from the sender… · the sender isn't streaming: Share Audio off, Send Audio off, or a different network.
Receiving data, but it is silent… · connection is fine; the sender's track has no signal.
…the host is not running this plugin. · the host never renders the channel. In AUM: give the channel a source, or use Receive Instrument as the source.
Syncing to the beat — buffering… · normal for a moment; if it persists, raise Latency under Advanced timing.
Receiving audio, but "Mute Received" / "Mute Output" is ON · un-mute the control it names (also shown when the master gain sits at silence).
Free limit reached… · second Receive instance without Unlimited Channels; unlock or remove the other instance.
No audio from the peer yet… · (Online) the peer left the room or isn't streaming.

Crackles or dropouts

Raise the Latency slider until clean, then add a small margin. On weak Wi-Fi move closer to the router; in Online mode try Mono send.

Feedback howling

The receiving track has a live mic input feeding through. Keep Host track muted (the default) in the Receive mixer, or use the Receive Instrument (no input at all).

I hear the track twice (sender side)

That is the dry signal plus the stream. Keep Mute Local Output on in the Send.

Online: "Room not found"

Code typo (codes are letters/numbers only; pasted spaces are removed automatically), or the room expired because it stayed empty. Create a fresh room.

Online: stuck on "waiting for peer"

The other side hasn't joined yet or joined a mistyped code. Re-share the code; both sides need Unlimited Channels.

Beat Sync won't lock (Online)

Is the host transport playing on both sides? Same tempo? The status line names what's missing. A tempo mismatch is shown as "you X / peer Y BPM".

Timing is stable but consistently early or late (LAN)

On the receiver, open Advanced timing and use Nudge (±100 ms with Bar Sync; in low-latency mode the slider is Delay-only — earlier audio doesn't exist yet). Sender-side there is also the Link Offset slider in the Send's gear settings.

Received audio sits slightly behind my local tracks (Cubasis and other hosts without delay compensation)

That constant offset is the host not compensating plugin latency — see §7. For grid-accurate recording: Bar Sync on, then Nudge to taste; afterwards shift the recorded region by the whole bars Bar Sync added.

9 · Frequently asked questions

Does LinkEffekt work with Ableton Live? Yes. Live 12.4+ receives Link Audio directly (§7), and LinkEffekt Send appears there as an input channel.

Does it work without a network? Yes — between apps on one device (channels tagged This device / This host) no network, permission or Link setup is needed (§2). Only cross-device LAN and Online need connectivity.

How many channels can I run? With Unlimited Channels: as many Send/Receive instances as your device handles; Online supports up to 8 streams per direction per host.

Does audio go through your servers? No. Online audio is peer-to-peer and DTLS-encrypted; the server only relays the room handshake. If a direct connection is impossible, an encrypted relay (TURN) forwards the still-encrypted stream.

Why is there always some delay? Physics: network transit plus safety buffering. LinkEffekt makes the delay stable and, with Beat Sync, musical (§7).

Which sample rates are supported? The host's rate (44.1/48/96 kHz…) on both sides; LinkEffekt converts internally. Online always transmits at 48 kHz/Opus.
